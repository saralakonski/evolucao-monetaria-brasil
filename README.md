### 📖 Miniguia de Estudo

### 1. Resumo Estruturado
#### A evolução monetária no Brasil

A história monetária brasileira foi marcada por diferentes moedas e reformas econômicas. Essas mudanças ocorreram principalmente como tentativa de controlar a inflação, simplificar valores monetários e recuperar a confiança da população na economia.

Ao longo do tempo, o Brasil utilizou moedas como Réis, Cruzeiro, Cruzeiro Novo, Cruzado, Cruzado Novo, Cruzeiro Real e Real. Muitas dessas substituições aconteceram em períodos de inflação elevada, quando os preços aumentavam rapidamente e o dinheiro perdia poder de compra.

Durante as décadas de 1980 e início dos anos 1990, o país enfrentou forte instabilidade econômica. Diversos planos econômicos foram implementados, mas muitos não conseguiram controlar a inflação de forma duradoura.

O Plano Real, implantado em 1994, foi um marco importante. Ele criou uma unidade de referência chamada URV antes da adoção do Real. Essa estratégia ajudou a reorganizar preços, salários e contratos, contribuindo para a estabilização da moeda e para a redução da inflação.

As moedas e cédulas antigas são importantes registros históricos. Elas revelam mudanças políticas, econômicas e culturais, além de preservarem memórias familiares e sociais.

### 📖 Study Mini-Guide

### 1. Structured summary
#### The Monetary Evolution in Brazil

Brazilian monetary history has been marked by several currencies and economic reforms. These changes were mainly implemented to control inflation, simplify monetary values, and restore public confidence in the economy.

Brazil used currencies such as Réis, Cruzeiro, Cruzeiro Novo, Cruzado, Cruzado Novo, Cruzeiro Real, and Real. Many of these transitions happened during periods of high inflation, when prices rose quickly and money lost purchasing power.

During the 1980s and early 1990s, Brazil experienced severe economic instability. Several economic plans were introduced, but many failed to control inflation in a lasting way.

The Real Plan, implemented in 1994, became a major turning point. It introduced a reference unit called URV before the adoption of the Real. This strategy helped reorganize prices, salaries, and contracts, contributing to currency stabilization and lower inflation.

Old coins and banknotes are important historical records. They reveal political, economic, and cultural changes while also preserving family and social memories.

### 2. Simplified Timeline

| Currency | Approximate Period | Historical Highlight |
| ------------- | ------------- | ------------- |
| Réis  | 16th century to 1942  | Brazil’s first monetary unit. |
| Cruzeiro  | 1942-1967 | Replaced the Réis.  |
| Cruzeiro Novo | 1967-1970  | Monetary simplification through the removal of zeros. |
| Cruzeiro  | 1970–1986 | Return of the Cruzeiro denomination.  |
| Cruzado  | 1986–1989  | Created during the Cruzado Plan. |
| Cruzado Novo | 1989–1990  | Another attempt at monetary reorganization. |
| Cruzeiro  | 1990–1993  | Temporary return of the Cruzeiro.  |
| Cruzeiro Real  | 1993–1994  | Transitional currency before the creation of the Real. |
| Real | 1994–present  | Current currency, created under the Real Plan. |


### 3. Glossary

| Concept | Definition |
| ------------- | ------------- |
| Inflation  | A continuous and widespread increase in the prices of goods and services. |
| Purchasing power  | The amount of goods and services a person can buy with their money.  |
| Monetary reform  |An official change in a country’s currency, usually linked to economic measures. |
| Currency conversion  | The process of converting values from an old currency to a new one.  |
| Indexation | The adjustment of prices, salaries, or contracts according to inflation indexes. |
| URV  | Real Value Unit, a reference unit used before the creation of the Real.  |
| Real Plan  | Economic plan implemented in 1994 to stabilize the economy and reduce inflation.  |
| Fiat money  | Money that has value because it is accepted and guaranteed by the government. |
| Numismatics  | The study and collection of coins, banknotes, and medals.  |
